# Operational Threat Intelligence

## What We Mean by Operational Threat Intelligence

Operational Threat Intelligence is intelligence that **directly enables action**.

It is not defined by the volume of indicators collected, the number of reports consumed, or the frequency of threat briefings produced. Instead, operational threat intelligence exists to **inform decisions**, **guide investigations**, and **produce durable detection and response outcomes**.

In SPARK, operational threat intelligence is intelligence that survives contact with reality — it can be hunted, validated, challenged, and ultimately converted into defensive capability.

---

## What Operational Threat Intelligence Is Not

Operational Threat Intelligence is **not**:

- A collection of raw IOCs without context
    
- Vendor-generated threat feeds consumed without analysis
    
- Strategic reporting intended solely for executive awareness
    
- Static PDFs that cannot be traced to detection outcomes
    

While these forms of intelligence may have value, they do not, on their own, change defensive posture or reduce risk at the operational level.

---

## Core Principles

SPARK is built on the following principles of operational threat intelligence:

### 1. Intelligence Must Be Actionable

Operational intelligence answers the question:  
**“What should an analyst do differently because of this?”**

If intelligence cannot be translated into a hunt, a validation exercise, or a detection strategy, it is incomplete.

---

### 2. Context Matters More Than Indicators

Indicators decay. Context endures.

Operational threat intelligence preserves:

- Adversary behavior
    
- Tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs)
    
- Assumptions and analytical reasoning
    
- Environmental relevance
    

Indicators are treated as **supporting evidence**, not the primary product.

---

### 3. Intelligence Is a Starting Point, Not a Conclusion

Operational threat intelligence does not assert certainty. It proposes hypotheses.

It is designed to be:

- Tested through threat hunting
    
- Validated or disproven by telemetry
    
- Refined through analyst review
    

Confidence increases through **analysis**, not ingestion.

---

### 4. Analyst Judgment Is Central

Operational threat intelligence is **analyst-owned**.

AI, automation, and tooling may assist with synthesis and scale, but they do not replace:

- Critical thinking
    
- Environmental awareness
    
- Risk-based decision-making
    

In SPARK, intelligence is always reviewable, editable, and explainable.

---

### 5. Intelligence Must Be Traceable End-to-End

Operational threat intelligence does not exist in isolation.

Every detection should be traceable back to:

- The intelligence that motivated it
    
- The hunt that validated it
    
- The findings that justified promotion
    

This traceability preserves intent, supports audits, and enables continuous improvement.

---

## The Operational Intelligence Lifecycle

In SPARK, operational threat intelligence follows a deliberate lifecycle:

1. **Intelligence Ingestion**  
    Curated reports, advisories, and research are captured with context and assessment.
    
2. **Threat Hunting**  
    Intelligence is translated into hypotheses and tested against real telemetry.
    
3. **Findings**  
    Results are documented, including uncertainty, scope, and confidence.
    
4. **Detection Strategy**  
    Validated behaviors are promoted into durable, explainable detections.
    

This lifecycle ensures intelligence does not stagnate — it evolves into capability.

---

## Why This Matters

Organizations do not fail because they lack intelligence.  
They fail because intelligence is **not operationalized**.

Operational threat intelligence closes the gap between knowing and defending. It ensures that time spent reading, researching, and analyzing threats results in measurable defensive outcomes.

SPARK exists to support this philosophy — by preserving context, enforcing structure, and keeping analysts in control from intelligence through detection.

---

_Operational Threat Intelligence is not about knowing more.  
It is about defending better._
